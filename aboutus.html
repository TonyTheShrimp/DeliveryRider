About Us

We are classmates and friends with common interests. We took advantage of the summer vacation to conduct a street survey of deliverymen. We also took advantage of travel opportunities to conduct face-to-face interviews with more than 60 deliverymen in several cities to understand their real lives.

I’m Tony. I first realized the huge difference between food delivery in China and the United States when I was at CMU summer school. I found that the delivery fee for a food delivery was more than 10 US dollars, which was really reluctant. Then I started to think about why food delivery in China is fast and cheap. I gradually became interested in studying the food delivery industry, and learned about the food deliverymen who were trapped in the algorithm and some of the problems they encountered. But it is not as good as first-hand information from the food deliverymen. Together with everyone, I talked face to face with dozens of food deliverymen, and I really had a lot of first-hand experience. For some topics, I can understand them better when I see the food deliverymen say it in person and see their expressions at the time. I hope that we can all understand and respect each other more. Only then can everyone live a better life.

I am Richard, a high school student in the United States. My school is located in a remote countryside in the United States, and I cannot order takeout. I miss the life in China where I can just order takeout and have it delivered within half an hour. So I feel that this opportunity to participate in the research on the life of takeout workers in China is very meaningful and rewarding.

I’m Winnie. My brother asked me to join the group just to make up the numbers, but it’s fun to research delivery drivers.
